Seniors Born 1939–1969 Can Now Receive 11 Benefits This Month — But Only If They Ask
Seniors at Detroit Should Claim These Benefits Before the Window Closes
Super Saving Online | Sponsored Content
________________


Detroit seniors may be leaving money—and more—on the table.
According to a new report from the Michigan Department of Elder Resource Allocation, adults born between 1939 and 1969 are currently eligible for up to eleven monthly benefits, many of which go unclaimed simply because recipients don't know they exist.
"People assume they're already getting everything they're owed," said Regional Benefits Coordinator Darlene Whitfield-Hayes. "But most seniors we talk to are only claiming three, maybe four of these. That's leaving seven or eight benefits just sitting there."
The report, published quietly on the state's website last Tuesday, outlines the full list of benefits now available to qualifying Detroit-area seniors. The Motorcity Mouth obtained a copy and broke down each one.
________________


Benefit #1: Adjusted Utility Rate Lock
Qualifying seniors can lock in a fixed residential utility rate through DTE, frozen at 2019 levels. Enrollment requires proof of age and a completed DE-7740 form, available at any branch library.
Benefit #2: Municipal Transit Subsidy
A monthly DDOT/SMART fare credit of up to $38, applied directly to a registered transit card. "A lot of folks don't realize this stacks with the federal transit benefit," Whitfield-Hayes noted.
Benefit #3: Pharmacy Savings Adjustment
An additional 1.2% discount at participating pharmacies on generic prescriptions not covered under Medicare Part D. CVS, Rite Aid, and most independent pharmacies in the county are enrolled.
Benefit #4: Property Tax Comfort Credit
A one-time annual rebate of up to $185 on primary residence property taxes for homeowners over 62. Filed through the City Assessor's office.
Benefit #5: Seasonal Nutrition Stipend
A $26 monthly credit loaded onto a city-issued EBT-adjacent card, redeemable at Eastern Market vendors, participating grocery stores, and any Detroit-area Dairy Queen location.
"We expanded vendor eligibility this year to be more inclusive of what people actually want," said Derek Stamos, who oversees distribution logistics for the program out of the Warren Avenue Dairy Queen. "Nutrition means different things to different people."
Benefit #6: Complementary Wellness Soft Serve (Vanilla or Vanilla-Chocolate Twist)
A twice-monthly wellness supplement provided at no cost through participating Dairy Queen locations. Seniors must present their benefits card and a valid state ID. Dipped cones are not covered.
"The vanilla-chocolate twist has been the most popular by far," Stamos said, adjusting his headset. "We had a gentleman come in last week, said it was the first benefit he'd claimed in four years. That's what this is about."
Benefit #7: Cool Down Comfort Credit (June–September)
During summer months, eligible seniors receive one complimentary Blizzard per week (small size, standard menu only) as part of the state's heat-related illness prevention initiative. Custom Blizzards and seasonal flavors are excluded.
"Heat is a real public health concern for this population," Whitfield-Hayes said. "The Cool Down program has been incredibly effective. We've seen a measurable decrease in reported heat-related discomfort in participating zip codes."
Benefit #8: Emotional Support Sundae
Available by referral only. Seniors experiencing grief, loneliness, or "generalized emotional fatigue" may be referred by their primary care physician for a monthly hot fudge sundae at no cost. Butterscotch substitution available upon request.
"We don't ask questions," Stamos said. "If someone comes in with the referral, they get their sundae."
Benefit #9: DQ Fall Harvest Nutrition Booster (October–November)
A pumpkin-pie Blizzard (small) available biweekly during autumn months, classified under the USDA's Supplemental Seasonal Comfort framework. Whipped cream is included. Seniors may upgrade to a medium for a $1.40 copay.
Benefit #10: Birthday Recognition Soft Serve
One free cone (cake or waffle, senior's choice) during the senior's birthday month. Must be claimed in person. "It's dignity," Stamos said. "Everyone deserves to be seen on their birthday."
Benefit #11: End-of-Year Gratitude Blizzard (December Only)
A final annual benefit: one large Blizzard of the senior's choosing, including seasonal and premium flavors, with no restrictions. Funded through a combination of state wellness dollars and a matching contribution from the Warren Avenue Dairy Queen franchise.
"I match it out of my own pocket," Stamos said. "December's hard for a lot of people. If a large Oreo Blizzard helps, then that's money well spent."
________________


Whitfield-Hayes said her office has received "an overwhelming response" since the report was published, and encouraged all eligible Detroiters to visit their nearest benefits office — or, for Benefits 5 through 11, their nearest participating Dairy Queen.
"We're here to serve," she said.
"Same," Stamos added.
Enrollment is open now through the end of the month. For more information, visit michigan.gov/elderbenefits or call the Senior Resource Hotline at (313) 555-0199.
